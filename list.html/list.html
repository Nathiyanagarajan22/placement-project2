History Of Olympics.......!

  • Origins of the Ancient Olympics
    • First recorded games in 776 BC
    • Held in Olympia, Greece
    • Dedicated to Zeus
    • Part of religious festivals
  • Religious and Cultural Significance
    • Linked to worship of Greek gods
    • Celebrated as a sacred truce period
    • Encouraged unity among Greek city-states
  • Events in Ancient Greece
    • Stadion race (200m sprint)
    • Wrestling
    • Chariot racing
    • Pentathlon (5 events)
  • Rules and Organization
    • Only free Greek men allowed to compete
    • No clothing worn during events
    • Judges called "Hellanodikai"
  • Decline and End of Ancient Olympics
    • Declined under Roman Empire
    • Banned by Emperor Theodosius I in 393 AD
    • Pagan traditions were discouraged
  • Modern Revival (1896)
    • Initiated by Pierre de Coubertin
    • First held in Athens, Greece
    • 13 countries participated
  • Key Figures in Olympic History
    • Pierre de Coubertin – founder of modern Olympics
    • Jesse Owens – 1936 Berlin Olympics hero
    • Usain Bolt – fastest man in history
  • Expansion of Sports and Countries
    • From 13 countries to over 200 nations
    • Winter Olympics introduced in 1924
    • Inclusion of women athletes
  • Major Historical Moments
    • 1968 Black Power salute
    • 1980 Moscow boycott
    • 2020 Tokyo Olympics during COVID-19
  • Changes in Rules and Technology
    • Electronic timing systems
    • Video replay for judging
    • Gender equality initiatives
